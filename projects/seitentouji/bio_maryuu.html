General Information

Personality

Maryuu is a spontaneous and childish android who almost always overreacts to any given situation. He's also quite belligerent and sort of wants to be a hero. That said, he loves the other androids who took him in, and tries his best to protect them in his own way. Especially big bro Raiden.

Abilities

Maryuu is able to render stories into Danmaku.

Maryuu's fighting style involves taking stories, and rendering them into danmaku. His recreations tend to be over the top elaborate, downright ridiculous, and/or amusing in a dark and highly destructive way.

This being said, he has familiars ('Laser Nexi') do the attacking. He has two types - a four slot laser nexus that is used to spawn lasers, and a spin-type danmaku spewing nexus.

Occupation

As of the ending of Adamantine Memory Blade, he is still living in the Village of Androids (Robot Suburbia)

Backstory

Maryuu (original name unknown) was a young child who met an early but sad fate. Once reincarnated, he inhabited a mechanical android body and adapted it to somewhat resemble his original appearance.

Character Design

Origin

Maryuu was created along with the main plot, as he plays the role of Stage 4 Boss and is required to fill in a number of very specific roles (i.e., he basically explains what's going on, etc.). He was the first of the three android cast members of Adamantine Memory Blade to have his name and story role finalized.

Name

Maryuu's name is completely arbitrary.

Design

Maryuu has a newer body model that resembles how we was when he was alive, except with countless extra features, such as the 'cool' blues patterns across his body and the unnecessary, likewise 'cool' plates used for his midsection. Of course, the material is not painted and is of premium quality metal. He still wrecks himself occasionally by doing reckless things (that was not a pun).

Relationships

Raiden

Maryuu adoringly refers to Raiden as 'Big Bro', though that is simply Maryuu latching onto someone out of loneliness. To be fair, the two get along well, and Raiden acts as mentor and teacher, as well as mechanic whenever Maryuu injures himself (which happens often).
